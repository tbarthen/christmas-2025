'Twas the night before Christmas in Superior's north end,
With a new furnace running, so the warm doesn't end.
The pop-up cards lined up all standing in rows,
A small paper village continuing to grow.
The cat was curled up, not a creature did stir,
Not even the north end rat covered in fur.
With Mom in her slippers and Dad in his chair,
The holiday spirit was simple but there.
No need for the fuss or the lights on the lawn,
Just two people together from dusk until dawn.
And we thought from out west as the holidays start,
Just know that you two have a place in our heart.
Love, Tom & Hema...
To Patty, Lisa, and Bob — and all your families —

hope your holidays are warm, peaceful,
and surrounded by the people who matter most.
